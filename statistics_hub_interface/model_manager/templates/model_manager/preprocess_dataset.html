Time Series Data Cleaning Process

This form facilitates the cleaning and preprocessing of time series data. The process involves the following steps:

  • Resampling: Adjusts the frequency of data points in your time series (e.g., from 30-second intervals to 1-minute intervals). This step helps in normalizing the data frequency for further analysis.
  • Aggregation: After resampling, data points are aggregated over the new time intervals using functions like mean, sum, max, or min. This step is essential for summarizing the data in a meaningful way.
  • Interpolation: This step fills in missing values in your time series. Different methods like linear, quadratic, or cubic interpolation can be used depending on how you wish to estimate these gaps.

By selecting the appropriate options below, you can customize how your time series data is processed to best suit your analysis needs.

Example Scenario

Imagine you have temperature data recorded every 30 seconds. However, you need to analyze the data at a 1-minute frequency. Additionally, some data points are missing.

Step 1 - Resampling: You choose to resample the data to a 1-minute frequency. This means if you originally had readings at 10:00:00, 10:00:30, and 10:01:00, they will now be grouped into intervals like 10:00:00 - 10:00:59, 10:01:00 - 10:01:59, etc.

Step 2 - Aggregation: For each 1-minute interval, you decide to use the mean (average) of the data points. If the readings were 22°C and 23°C in the 10:00:00 - 10:00:59 interval, the aggregated value for this minute will be 22.5°C.

Step 3 - Interpolation: For any minute where data is missing, you choose a linear interpolation method. If there's no reading at 10:02:00, but the readings at 10:01:00 and 10:03:00 are 22.5°C and 23°C respectively, the interpolated value for 10:02:00 would be estimated as 22.75°C.
